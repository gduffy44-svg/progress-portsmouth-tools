Lower Your Electric Bill.

What actually moves the needle, ranked by how much it saves.

Need help with a bill right now?
Tier 01 — Highest impact

If energy bills are challenging your budget

Two programs, big savings. Income limits are higher than most people expect — check before assuming you don't qualify.

Apply for the Electric Assistance Program

A discount of up to 86% on the first 750 kWh of your monthly Eversource bill. Year-round. Not a loan.

  • Family of 1≤ $47,604
  • Family of 2≤ $62,252
  • Family of 3≤ $76,900
  • Family of 4≤ $91,548
  • Family of 5≤ $106,195
Apply through Community Action Partnership (CAPHR)
4 Cutts St, Portsmouth · (603) 436-3896

Discount applies whether your supplier is Portsmouth Community Power, Eversource default, or a third-party supplier. Renters and homeowners both qualify.

Free NH Saves home energy audit

An auditor visits your home, installs LEDs, weatherstripping, and smart strips on the spot, and identifies rebate-eligible upgrades like insulation and heat pumps.

Funded by the System Benefits Charge already on your monthly utility bill — you've already paid for it.

Renters: ask your landlord. NH Saves often covers most of the upgrade cost, so many landlords will agree.

Learn more · nhsaves.com

Income-eligible households can have weatherization upgrades fully covered through the Home Energy Assistance program. Eligibility differs from EAP — the agency will check both at the same appointment.

Tier 02 — Medium impact

For every Portsmouth household

Three actions that work regardless of income or housing situation.

Check your electricity supply rate

Eversource delivers your power; a separate supplier sets the per-kWh rate — the bigger half of your bill. Switching is free, takes minutes, and never interrupts service.

Portsmouth residents are defaulted into Portsmouth Community Power. Through July 31, 2026, the CPCNH default rate is 14.663¢/kWh — currently higher than Eversource's 11.3¢/kWh. The City Energy Advisory Committee notes that opting out to Eversource saves the average 650-kWh-per-month household about $22/month for this rate period.

Customers can move between Eversource and Community Power without penalty, and rates reset Feb 1 and Aug 1 — worth comparing each cycle. Community Power's enduring value is local control over the energy mix and the ability to "opt up" to higher-renewable tiers, not a guaranteed lower rate.

Opt in/out · communitypowernh.gov/portsmouth
Or call 1-866-603-7697
Licensed third-party suppliers · energy.nh.gov

Tune up your AC before summer

According to DOE, a clogged filter alone can drive electricity use up by 5–15%. A spring tune-up addresses additional inefficiencies — refrigerant levels, dirty coils, duct leaks — that compound the loss. Schedule the inspection in late spring before contractors get booked.

Can't afford a tune-up? The two highest-impact DIY steps are: change the filter (~$15) and clear leaves and debris from the outdoor compressor unit so air flows through.

Set the thermostat at 78°F when home

Each degree above 72°F saves about 3% on cooling cost. Set higher when out.

If 78°F feels too warm, a ceiling fan can make a room feel about 4°F cooler at a fraction of the electricity. Run fans only when the room is occupied — fans cool people, not air.

Tier 03 — Small but easy

Quick wins, no special equipment

Each is small on its own. Together they shave maybe 5–10% off a summer cooling bill.

  • Replace HVAC filters Every 1–3 months. About $15.
  • Close blinds On south- and west-facing windows in the afternoon.
  • Add weatherstripping At door and window gaps. Reversible — renter-friendly.
  • Run fans only in occupied rooms Fans cool people, not air.
  • Run laundry & dishwasher after 8 p.m. When grid demand drops.
  • Unplug idle electronics Or use a smart strip. Phantom load adds up.
Tier 04 — Emergency contacts

If you're behind on a bill

Call before a shutoff notice arrives — payment plans are easier to set up early than late.

  • Eversource billing (800) 662-7764 Ask about payment arrangements and EAP enrollment.
  • Community Action Partnership (CAPHR) (603) 436-3896 4 Cutts St, Portsmouth · Fuel Assistance, EAP, Weatherization, and Neighbor Helping Neighbor (last-resort fund for households who don't qualify for LIHEAP). Formed by the merger of Rockingham Community Action and Southern NH Services.
  • 211 NH 211 Cooling centers during heat advisories. Local referrals to other support.
Why bills keep climbing

Three forces are pushing New England electricity rates up: growing demand from data centers and the electrification of heating and transportation, rising costs to upgrade and maintain the grid, and current global oil and gas market disruptions. Federal forecasts project U.S. residential rates rising ~4% in 2026 on top of 2025's 11.5% increase. The actions above won't reverse the trend — but they can blunt its impact.
