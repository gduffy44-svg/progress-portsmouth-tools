Intake protocol

Progress Portsmouth received unsolicited expert input from a credentialed planner residing in another New Hampshire community. The input addressed multiple aspects of the Housing Action Plan and surrounding municipal policy. Progress Portsmouth triaged the input through three lenses:

  1. Substantive policy or technical contributions that translate cleanly into action items or amendments — included below.
  2. Contested policy positions that deserve consideration but cannot be silently absorbed into the plan — recorded but not drafted as items.
  3. Personnel or process critiques that fall outside the scope of the action plan — referred to the appropriate body or recorded for context.

The four items below resulted from the first lens. Any of them may be accepted as drafted, accepted with modifications, or declined. The review questions at the end of this page guide the committee and Planning Department's response.

Source of input: Tom Morgan, AICP — credentialed planner, resident of another New Hampshire community. Input received April 30, 2026.

Review questions

For each proposed item, the Housing Committee and Planning Department are asked to consider:

  1. Should the proposed item be incorporated into the Housing Action Plan as drafted?
  2. Should the proposed item be incorporated with modifications? If so, what modifications?
  3. Should the proposed item be declined, and on what basis?
  4. If accepted, what canonical ID should the item receive within the existing category structure?
