The process — three phases, six sessions
1The Sweep
Initial sort
Walk all 148 items. Two minutes each. Three buckets only. No deep dives — move fast.
Sessions 1–2 · ~80 items/session
2Depth Triage
Active list only
Apply the four capital flow questions to each remaining item. Deliberate. Rank within categories.
Sessions 3–5 · ~25 items/session
3Sequencing
Order the work
Sequence top priorities against dependencies and implementation paths. Assign tracks.
Session 6 · Final output to Council
Phase 1 — Three buckets
✅
Pursue — Year 1
High priority, actionable, within HC scope. Goes to active list for Phase 2.
🕐
Pursue — Later
Important but not Year 1. Deferred — may depend on upstream items.
🅿️
Park
Out of scope, superseded, already complete, or not actionable this term.
Phase 2 — Four questions per item
1
Who does this affect?
Homeowner · Small developer · Nonprofit · Multiple · No specific actor
2
Does it change a decision or reduce friction?
Changes decision · Reduces friction · Neither
3
What's the city's actual leverage?
Direct (city acts alone) · Conditional (actor must respond) · Outside city control
4
What happens if this is delayed two years?
Capital goes elsewhere · Project stalls · Compliance deadline · No material effect
Phase 1 disciplineTwo minutes per item. No deliberation — save disagreements for Phase 2. Flag items needing more time; don't resolve them in the sweep.
Facilitator roleFacilitator calls each item, takes a show of hands on bucket, notes any flags. A designated committee member flags dependency conflicts in real time. Move on.
Reference toolsHAP Explorer for item detail. Capital Flow module for Phase 2 background. Dependency map for sequencing. All optional — sessions run without them.
