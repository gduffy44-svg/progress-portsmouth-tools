A practical test for HAP triage

Someone has to fund and build it.

It's worth stating the obvious: for every desired outcome the Housing Committee identifies, someone has to fund and build a project. Otherwise nothing changes.

Here's a simple test you can apply against any item in the HAP that will help you assign priority.

01
Permanently Affordable Workforce Housing
Mission-driven nonprofits build income-restricted multifamily with public, philanthropic, and LIHTC capital under regulated affordability rules.
Active in Portsmouth
02
Resident-Led Gentle Density
Owner-occupants build ADUs and small conversions with personal savings and state ADU program capital under permissive small-scale zoning.

03
Neighborhood Builder Program
Local small-scale investors build missing-middle infill with private and community capital under rules designed to enable small-builder economics.
Aspirational — not yet built

How the test works

Score each HAP item against the three pathways: strengthens, weakens, or no effect. The aggregate shows where your priorities concentrate — and which pathways might be quietly underbuilt.

Print or download your results to share with the Housing Committee or Planning staff.
