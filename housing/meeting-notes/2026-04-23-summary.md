## Discussion summary

### 1. Last year's action items and accomplishments (Tabor)

Co-chair Tabor reviewed the prior year's Housing Committee accomplishments:

- **Sherburne School** — Committee spent 20 meetings providing public input to the City Manager and helping craft an RFP for redevelopment as housing. Portsmouth Housing Authority was selected for the project.
- **City property** — Committee walked and reviewed City property to identify parcels that might support housing. Continued review of additional opportunities to partner with PHA properties.
- **Housing Trust Fund** — Committee championed and created the Housing Trust Fund; current balance approximately $500,000.
- **Gateway Neighborhood Overlay District** — First GNOD created off Commerce Way, with 1-acre parcel available for development.
- **Further zoning** — Building on the prior Land Use Board (chaired by Councilor Moreau) which rezoned Commercial areas to allow Mixed Use, the Committee is now exploring how zoning can be reimagined to allow more residential use, especially multi-family.

### 2. Housing Action Plan framing

Councilor Cook's successful motion at the February 17, 2026 City Council meeting directed adoption of Progress Portsmouth's Housing Action Plan. The Committee discussed the path forward:

- The goal is a **systematic plan**, not opportunistic action.
- Working with staff, the Committee will deliver priorities to City Council by **July 2026**.
- Every member's voice will be included in determining the most important near- and long-term actions that are feasible.

**Tabor identified five key issues** the Housing Action Plan should address:

1. **Permitting** — relieve land use board friction
2. **Incentives and financial tools** — fund the Housing Trust Fund
3. **City-owned land opportunities** — e.g., City Hall lower lot
4. **Partnerships and capacity**
5. **Education and outreach** with data collection on best practices around the country (in coordination with Housing New Hampshire)

### 3. RSA 79-E discussion

The Committee discussed at length what opportunities the new 79-E might offer:

- 79-E provides a tax freeze for a set period on historic properties adapted for reuse as housing, and may have coastal resilience project applications.
- However, **79-E does not apply to older properties** that might be kept as workforce housing with an incentive to do so.
- The Committee **suggested a work session to create an income-restricted structure** to present to Council.
- **Next step:** discuss with Legal and Tax Assessor.

### 4. Housing Action Plan priorities — process for review

The Committee discussed how to review all 147 Progress Portsmouth recommendations to identify a practical plan:

- **Britz** (Director of Planning and Sustainability) presented the rationale his four-person Planning team (Britz, Casella, Gilbo, plus one) used in selecting **26 feasible actions** for near- and long-term action.
- The Committee agreed each member would bring their **"top 3" and "top 10"** from that list — plus any items from the original 147-item plan that should also be prioritized — for discussion at the May meeting.
