## Discussion summary

Anderson asked what the goal of the Housing Action Plan priorities is, noting that the previous Committee had set a goal of adding workforce / below-market-rate units. He flagged twelve items from the 147 Progress Portsmouth recommendations he felt warranted inclusion alongside the highlighted shortlist (ten items listed in the minutes; the two unlisted items were not clarified). He raised that "workforce housing" affordability needs more specific definition.

Kelley and Moreau defined the immediate goal as determining priorities and implementation responsibilities for recommendation to City Council by the July 2026 deadline, with the balance of the Committee term spent implementing those actions. Stith confirmed the Zoning Audit (Z-21) is now underway and will be reflected in both the Housing Action Plan and the Master Plan.

Moreau, Samonas, and Migirditch discussed tax relief as an actionable priority. Moreau explained that Portsmouth does not have municipal authority to provide property tax relief outside the categories defined by RSA 674 (solar, elderly, veterans, disabled). She identified creative interpretation of RSA 79-E as a viable path.

Migirditch proposed bundling related items into thematic categories (e.g., zoning-related items grouped under a Zoning priority).

Weinrieb and Kelley framed the boundary of the Committee's work as actions within the City's control — not land costs, construction costs, infrastructure costs, or demand.

Samonas raised three meta points:
1. Completed items (the zoning audit, the creation of the Housing Trust Fund) should not be listed as priorities.
2. Only 2% of city land is available; the Plan should focus on best use of available land, including non-conforming examples, to enable workforce housing.
3. The Housing Action Plan should address the needs of audiences with no current inventory match — e.g., older couples seeking to downsize who are not candidates for subsidized senior apartment housing.

Samonas and Kelley raised sharing recommendations that don't fit Portsmouth's Housing Action Plan with surrounding communities that might support additional workforce housing.
